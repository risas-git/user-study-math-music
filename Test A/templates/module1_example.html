Module 1 Reference & Worked Examples

Example 1: Order of Operations (Arithmetic Precedence)

Evaluate $240 \div (15 - 3) + 18 \times 4$

Step 1 (Parentheses): $15 - 3 = 12 \rightarrow 240 \div 12 + 18 \times 4$
Step 2 (Division & Multiplication): $240 \div 12 = 20$ and $18 \times 4 = 72 \rightarrow 20 + 72$
Step 3 (Addition): $20 + 72 = 92$
Answer: $92$

Example 2: Common Denominators & Fraction Operations

Evaluate $\frac{3}{4} + \frac{5}{6} - \frac{2}{3}$

Step 1 (Find LCD): Least Common Denominator of $4, 6, 3$ is $12$.
Step 2 (Convert fractions): $\frac{3 \times 3}{12} + \frac{5 \times 2}{12} - \frac{2 \times 4}{12} = \frac{9}{12} + \frac{10}{12} - \frac{8}{12}$
Step 3 (Combine numerators): $\frac{9 + 10 - 8}{12} = \frac{11}{12}$
Answer: $\frac{11}{12}$

Example 3: Complex Fraction Division

Simplify $\frac{\frac{4}{9}}{\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{6}}$

Step 1 (Denominator Multiplication): $\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{6} = \frac{10}{18} = \frac{5}{9}$
Step 2 (Invert and Multiply): $\frac{4}{9} \div \frac{5}{9} = \frac{4}{9} \times \frac{9}{5} = \frac{4}{5}$
Answer: $\frac{4}{5}$

Example 4: Rational Algebraic Equations

Solve for $x$: $\frac{2}{x+3} + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{5}{x+3}$

Step 1 (Group like terms): Subtract $\frac{2}{x+3}$ from both sides $\rightarrow \frac{1}{x} = \frac{5 - 2}{x+3} = \frac{3}{x+3}$
Step 2 (Cross Multiply): $x+3 = 3x \rightarrow 2x = 3 \rightarrow x = \frac{3}{2}$
Answer: $x = \frac{3}{2}$
