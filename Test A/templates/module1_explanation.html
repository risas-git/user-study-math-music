Module 1: Mixed Operations & Rational Expressions

This module evaluates your proficiency with arithmetic precedence, fraction manipulation, complex rational expressions, and algebraic equations. Review the core rules below before starting the exercise.

1. Order of Operations (BODMAS / PEMDAS)

Always perform calculations in the following strict order:
1️⃣ Brackets / Parentheses first.
2️⃣ Orders / Exponents.
3️⃣ Division & Multiplication (from left to right).
4️⃣ Addition & Subtraction (from left to right).
Example: $5421 - 382 \times 4 + 120 \div 6 \rightarrow 5421 - 1528 + 20 = 3913$.

2. Rational Fractions & Common Denominators

To add or subtract fractions, find the Least Common Denominator (LCD) first: $$\frac{a}{b} + \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ad + bc}{bd}$$ Example: $\frac{3}{4} + \frac{5}{6} - \frac{2}{3} = \frac{9}{12} + \frac{10}{12} - \frac{8}{12} = \frac{11}{12}$.

3. Complex Rational Division

When dividing complex rational fractions, invert the denominator fraction and multiply: $$\frac{\frac{a}{b}}{\frac{c}{d}} = \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{d}{c}$$

4. Rational Algebraic Equations

Cross-multiply by the LCD $(x-a)(x-b)$ to eliminate denominators. Always verify that solutions do not cause a zero in any denominator (extraneous roots).
