Module 1 Reference & Worked Examples

Example 1: Order of Operations (Arithmetic Precedence)

Evaluate $360 \div (18 - 6) + 14 \times 5$

Step 1 (Parentheses): $18 - 6 = 12 \rightarrow 360 \div 12 + 14 \times 5$
Step 2 (Division & Multiplication): $360 \div 12 = 30$ and $14 \times 5 = 70 \rightarrow 30 + 70$
Step 3 (Addition): $30 + 70 = 100$
Answer: $100$

Example 2: Common Denominators & Fraction Operations

Evaluate $\frac{2}{5} + \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{2}$

Step 1 (Find LCD): Least Common Denominator of $5, 4, 2$ is $20$.
Step 2 (Convert fractions): $\frac{2 \times 4}{20} + \frac{3 \times 5}{20} - \frac{1 \times 10}{20} = \frac{8}{20} + \frac{15}{20} - \frac{10}{20}$
Step 3 (Combine numerators): $\frac{8 + 15 - 10}{20} = \frac{13}{20}$
Answer: $\frac{13}{20}$

Example 3: Complex Fraction Division

Simplify $\frac{\frac{3}{8}}{\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{5}{6}}$

Step 1 (Denominator Multiplication): $\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{5}{6} = \frac{5}{24}$
Step 2 (Invert and Multiply): $\frac{3}{8} \div \frac{5}{24} = \frac{3}{8} \times \frac{24}{5} = \frac{3 \times 3}{5} = \frac{9}{5}$
Answer: $\frac{9}{5}$

Example 4: Rational Algebraic Equations

Solve for $x$: $\frac{3}{x+2} + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{6}{x+2}$

Step 1 (Group like terms): Subtract $\frac{3}{x+2}$ from both sides $\rightarrow \frac{1}{x} = \frac{6 - 3}{x+2} = \frac{3}{x+2}$
Step 2 (Cross Multiply): $x+2 = 3x \rightarrow 2x = 2 \rightarrow x = \frac{2}{3}$
Answer: $x = \frac{2}{3}$
