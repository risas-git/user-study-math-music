Module 2 Reference & Worked Examples

Example 1: Polynomial Derivatives & Power Rule

Find the derivative of $y = 4x^4 + 3x^2 - 5x + 9$

Step 1 (Apply Power Rule): $\frac{d}{dx}(4x^4) = 16x^3$, $\frac{d}{dx}(3x^2) = 6x$, $\frac{d}{dx}(-5x) = -5$
Step 2 (Constant Derivative): $\frac{d}{dx}(9) = 0$
Answer: $y' = 16x^3 + 6x - 5$

Example 2: Definite Integration & Fundamental Theorem

Evaluate $\int_1^2 (6x^2 - 4x) \, dx$

Step 1 (Find Antiderivative): $F(x) = \int (6x^2 - 4x) dx = 2x^3 - 2x^2$
Step 2 (Evaluate Limits $F(2) - F(1)$): $(2(8) - 2(4)) - (2(1) - 2(1)) = (16 - 8) - (2 - 2) = 8 - 0 = 8$
Answer: $8$

Example 3: Product Rule & Logarithms

Find the derivative of $y = x^4 \cdot \ln(x)$

Step 1 (Identify $u$ and $v$): $u = x^4 \Rightarrow u' = 4x^3$ and $v = \ln(x) \Rightarrow v' = \frac{1}{x}$
Step 2 (Apply $u'v + uv'$): $4x^3 \ln(x) + x^4 \cdot \frac{1}{x} = 4x^3 \ln(x) + x^3 = x^3(4\ln(x) + 1)$
Answer: $y' = x^3(4\ln(x) + 1)$

Example 4: Integration by Parts

Evaluate $\int 2x e^{2x} \, dx$

Step 1 (Set $u$ and $dv$): $u = x \Rightarrow du = dx$ and $dv = 2e^{2x} dx \Rightarrow v = e^{2x}$
Step 2 (Apply $uv - \int v du$): $x e^{2x} - \int e^{2x} dx = x e^{2x} - \frac{1}{2}e^{2x} + C = e^{2x}(x - \frac{1}{2}) + C$
Answer: $e^{2x}(x - \frac{1}{2}) + C$
